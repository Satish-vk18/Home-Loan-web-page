FQ's

A home loan is a financial solution designed to help individuals purchase, construct, or renovate their dream homes. Offered by banks and financial institutions, home loans allow borrowers to acquire property by providing funds upfront, which are repaid in monthly installments over a specific tenure. These loans typically come with flexible repayment options and competitive interest rates, either fixed or floating, based on the borrower’s preference.To apply, borrowers need to meet eligibility criteria, including age, income, credit score, and employment stability. Lenders also evaluate the property's value and legal status before sanctioning the loan. Most home loans cover up to 75-90% of the property's cost, requiring borrowers to contribute the remaining amount as a down payment.Home loans often offer tax benefits under sections 80C and 24 of the Income Tax Act, making them an attractive option for homebuyers seeking financial ease.

What documents are required to apply for a home loan?

You will need identity proof (e.g., Aadhaar, PAN card), address proof, income proof (salary slips or IT returns), property documents, and bank statements for the last 6 months. Additional documents may be requested depending on the lender.

What is the minimum credit score required for a home loan?

Most lenders require a credit score of 650-750 or higher to approve a home loan. A higher credit score increases your chances of getting better interest rates.

How long does it take to get a home loan approved?

The approval process typically takes 7-15 business days, depending on the lender and the completeness of your application and documentation.
